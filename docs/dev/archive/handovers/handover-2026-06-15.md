## The model (the ruleset to curate against) — see the spec for detail
- **`HAS → CAN GET → HAS THE MEANS TO`**, one uniform chain/trigger, bounded by HAS (small list, not the whole DB).
- **Source side — FOUR base objects (PERMANENT, forward, drive generation):**
  `enables` (constructive; **tech here**) · `disables` (destructive ban — instances DESTROYED, rebuild-on-repeal) ·
  `obsoletes` (passive supersede — **target decides fate** via `whenObsolete`) · `replaces` (succession; overrides
  obsolete-stay). **CAN GET = enables − (disables ∪ obsoletes ∪ replaces)** over HAS.
- **Target side — `requires` (REVERSIBLE gate):** mostly POSITIVE means (`all`/`any` presence + `min`/`max` counts),
  with an optional NEGATIVE (`disableIfAny`/`disableIfAll`) for reversible DORMANCY (pseudobuildings). Split
  `requires.build` (one-time) vs `requires.operate` (continuous/dormancy); units use `build` only. **Tech may sit in
  `requires.build`** for the per-candidate confirm (tech-tree AND/OR) — never as a generation driver.
- **Instance fate:** `disables`→destroyed · requires-negative→dormant · positive-`requires`-fail→dormant ·
  `obsoletes`→target decides · `replaces`→replaced. *(OPEN: owner to give a final yes/no on this table.)*
- **Logic:** two stages — combinator (`all`/`any` × polarity) then conditions (presence, or `min`/`max` count).
  Predictable ⇒ cacheable (optional layer). **`tally`** module holds cross-city counts (renamed from "statistics";
  `census` collides with the benchmark project); `min`/`max` are its only consumers, prefix-routed, volumetric-ready
  via `min(BONUS_COAL,1)`. Greying = resources in `requires`; pedia keeps "Obsoleted by", no "potentially disabled by".
